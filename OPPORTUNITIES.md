# The Rooiberg Wander — Opportunities for Improvement

A content-gap analysis against high-quality Limpopo competitors, with concrete add-ons to
consider. Prepared 2026-06-22.

## Competitors reviewed (via fagalavoet.co.za/limpopo)

| Trail | Format | Why it's a useful benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| **The Old Salt Trail** (Soutpansberg) | 5-day / 4-night premium **slackpacking**, R12,500 pp all-in | Closest format match. Very deep, structured content. |
| **Africa on Foot Wilderness Trail** (Greater Kruger) | 3-night guided **Big-5 walking safari**, ~R5,775 pp/night | The "walking safari" benchmark — guiding, wildlife, what-to-expect. |
| **Lindani** (Waterberg) | Self-catering lodges + trail network | Closest geographically; self-catering peer; downloadable maps. |

## What we already do better than most competitors (keep / lean into)

- **Detailed cancellation & refund policy** — none of the three show one. Real trust signal.
- **Per-day elevation profiles** — Old Salt Trail has them; Africa on Foot does not. We now match the best.
- **Exclusive private-use model** — competitors run shared/combined groups (min 4, strangers
  combined). "The whole reserve and lodges are yours" is a genuine differentiator — say it louder.
- **100% malaria-free** — a real edge over Greater-Kruger walking safaris (which are malarial).
- **Per-group pricing transparency** + clear included/excluded.
- **Honest route note, clean FAQ, provisions (Checkers/Sixty60) and transfers (EZ Shuttle) info.**

## Gaps & opportunities (prioritised)

### HIGH — booking-critical / trust

1. **Availability & how-to-book clarity.** Old Salt Trail lists specific 2026 departure dates;
   Africa on Foot lists set departure days. We currently show "booking coming soon" with no dates,
   lead times or season window.
   - *Add-on:* an availability statement ("private departures year-round, subject to availability;
     book X weeks ahead"), a simple availability calendar or "request a date" flow, and turn on the
     live booking widget. Even before online payment is live, show **how booking works** end-to-end.

2. **Real contact details + fast channels.** Every competitor shows a **phone number and office
   hours**; we only have a placeholder email. SA travellers expect **WhatsApp**.
   - *Add-on:* real phone + **WhatsApp click-to-chat**, office hours, expected response time, and a
     visible "Contact" point in the header/footer. (Needs the operator's real number/email.)

3. **Per-day summary table (distance · ascent · difficulty · time).** Old Salt Trail's clean table
   (route / distance / elevation gain / difficulty) is the gold standard; ours is prose + profiles.
   - *Add-on:* a compact table per walking day with **distance, total ascent, a difficulty rating,
     and rough hours on foot / start time** (e.g. "early start ~06:30, 5–6 hrs"). The elevation-gain
     numbers can be read off the profiles we already have.

4. **"What to bring" / kit list.** All three at least say "packing list provided on booking", and
   list day-pack essentials. We deliberately left this out.
   - *Add-on:* a short kit list — walking shoes, layered clothing for the Waterberg, sun protection,
     a daypack, water bottle, headlamp, personal medication, neutral colours for game areas — plus
     "a full packing list is sent when you book." Also doubles as an SEO page ("what to pack for a
     slackpacking trail").

5. **Accommodation detail per lodge.** Old Salt Trail specifies room types, beds, bathrooms and hot
   water per night; we have character + a shared amenities list only.
   - *Add-on:* per-lodge specifics — how many it sleeps, bed/bedding setup, bathrooms & hot water,
     power (solar/gas), and a line on the kitchen/braai. (Needs operator detail + lodge photos.)

6. **Photo gallery.** Competitors carry galleries (Old Salt Trail ~11 images). We have a hero,
   three lodge images and a few banners.
   - *Add-on:* a gallery per lodge and for the trail (trail, camps, food/braai, wildlife, guides).
     Highest-impact single upgrade. (Needs real photography — especially **of the lodges/rooms**.)

### MEDIUM — depth, differentiation, SEO

7. **"Wildlife you may see" (and birding).** Old Salt Trail lists species; we only imply Big 5.
   We already have lion, leopard, giraffe and kudu photos.
   - *Add-on:* a wildlife & birding section (Big 5 status, plains game, notable birds). Strong for
     search/AI ("Big 5 walking trail Waterberg", "what animals will I see").

8. **When to come / weather / seasons.** None of the three do this well — an easy way to out-class
   them. *Add-on:* a "Best time to walk" section (Waterberg seasons, summer vs winter temps, what
   to expect, green vs dry season). Good evergreen SEO.

9. **Getting here (proper directions).** We say "2.5 hrs from Joburg" + drive to Rotavi. Competitors
   add nearest town, road type and (sometimes) GPS.
   - *Add-on:* directions from Joburg/Pretoria, nearest town (Bela-Bela/Rooiberg), **nearest
     airport** (OR Tambo/Lanseria), road conditions (tar/gravel, clearance?), GPS pin for Rotavi
     Lodge, and the parking/secure-vehicle note we already have.

10. **Guide credentials.** We say "qualified, armed trail guides"; the brief references **FGASA Lead
    Trails Guide**. Big-5-on-foot buyers care about this.
    - *Add-on:* name the credentials — FGASA Trails Guide / Lead Trails Guide, rifle competency,
      first-aid — and a short "meet your guides". (Needs operator confirmation.)

11. **Fitness & preparation.** Old Salt Trail and Africa on Foot give walk durations and a candid
    fitness warning. We say "moderate to challenging" only.
    - *Add-on:* "Are you fit enough?" guidance, daily walking hours/timing, terrain notes (kloofs,
      donga, ascent), and light training tips.

12. **Age / suitability policy.** Competitors specify (12+, 16+, under-18 with guardian). We say
    nothing. *Add-on:* a minimum-age / suitability line.

13. **The reserve & conservation story.** Old Salt Trail leans on its EWT partnership. We mention
    conservation levies and RoiSan Reserve NPC but tell no story.
    - *Add-on:* a short "About the reserve / conservation" section — RoiSan Reserve, the Big-5
      malaria-free wilderness, what the conservation levies support. Builds E-E-A-T and trust.

14. **Social proof / testimonials.** None on the three reviewed — but premium brands (&Beyond,
    Singita, Leopard Trail) all use them, and it's a conversion lever.
    - *Add-on:* a testimonials section once you have guest reviews; link Google reviews when live.

### LOWER — nice-to-have / later

15. **Self-catering guidance.** Since guests cater, spell out what each kitchen has (fridge/freezer,
    gas/electric, braai, crockery), wood/gas supply, and simple meal-planning tips. (Lindani details
    kitchen amenities; we just list "equipped kitchen".)
16. **Downloadable resources.** A trail PDF and/or **GPX route file** (Lindani uses Trailforks +
    PDFs). Good for serious hikers and SEO.
17. **"Per person" value framing.** We're per-group; competitors are per-person. At R60,000 for up
    to 10 that's **~R6,000 pp** — versus ~R5,775 pp **per night** at Africa on Foot. Add a subtle
    "works out to roughly R6,000 per person for a group of 10" to make the value obvious.
18. **Trust badges / associations.** FGASA, SATSA, Waterberg Biosphere, secure-payment (Paystack)
    badges once live.
19. **Blog / content engine (SEO).** The roadmap is already noted: "what is slackpacking", "best
    time to visit the Waterberg", "what to pack", "Big 5 on foot — is it safe?", "slackpacking vs
    walking safari", "malaria-free safaris near Johannesburg".
20. **Video.** A short hero/trail film when footage exists — premium operators lead with it.
